Kosovo's Landscapes

Mountains

Sharr Mountains

The Sharr Mountains offer breathtaking views and diverse hiking trails, with peaks reaching over 2,500 meters in height.

Bjeshkët e Nemuna

Known as the Albanian Alps, these mountains feature dramatic limestone peaks and pristine alpine lakes.

Winter Sports

Kosovo's mountains provide excellent opportunities for skiing and other winter sports activities.

Gjeravica Peak

Kosovo's highest peak offering breathtaking panoramic views.

Rugova Mountains

Dramatic cliffs and deep valleys in western Kosovo.

Kopaonik Mountains

Beautiful mountain range with rich mineral deposits.

Water Features

Mirusha Waterfalls

A spectacular series of 13 lakes and waterfalls, creating one of Kosovo's most beautiful natural attractions.

White Drin Waterfall

The impressive White Drin Waterfall is one of Kosovo's most visited natural landmarks.

Alpine Lakes

Numerous pristine alpine lakes dot the mountain landscape, providing crucial freshwater resources.

Mirusha Falls

Series of beautiful waterfalls and pools in central Kosovo.

Gazivoda Lake

Large artificial lake with stunning mountain backdrop.

Lumbardhi River

Crystal clear mountain river flowing through Prizren.

Caves and Canyons

Gadime Cave

Famous for its unique aragonite crystals and marble formations, extending several kilometers underground.

Rugova Canyon

One of the deepest and most impressive canyons in Europe, offering spectacular views and climbing opportunities.

Marble Cave

A natural wonder featuring stunning marble formations and underground streams.

Radavc Cave

Ancient cave system with impressive stalactites and stalagmites.

Peja Cave

Historic cave with archaeological significance.

Gadime Cave

Famous for its unique aragonite crystal formations.
